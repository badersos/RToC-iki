Various Forms of Heavenly Punishment

Heavenly Punishment is a phenomenon faced by all those who defy the heavens.

Heavenly Punishment is a phenomenon faced by all those who defy the heavens. All cultivators face some form of Heavenly Punishment during their advancement.

Known Types of Heavenly Punishment

There exists many kinds of Heavenly Punishments across the Three Thousand Worlds. Though the heavenly tribulations belong to the domain of Owner of Heavenly Punishment,[1] it is unknown if the other Heavenly Punishments originate from this being. It is later discovered that Heavenly Punishments originate from going against the hearts of the greater population.[2]

Heavenly Rejection (天拒)

Heavenly Rejection is a phenomena that occurs exclusively to all Enders. During the 7th Star Ritual of the Qi Refining stage, a Heaven Tribe cultivator has to make a ritual to the heavens during the night. The purpose of this is to offer a ritual to seven of the twenty-eight celestial stars governing the spiritual nature of heaven and earth that will help the cultivator in their Qi Building stage. This ritual will fail without a direct connection to the stars.[3]

A Heavenly Rejection manifests in the form of dark clouds spontaneously appearing during an Ender's 7th Star Ritual. So long as the Ender intended to perform the ritual, the clouds would persist until dawn, when the stars would recede. External help would disqualify the ritual, forcing the Ender to remove the obstruction with their own power.[4] As their cultivation remained low, many Enders throughout history were unable to perform the 7th Star Ritual, eventually dying of old age.

Under extraordinary circumstances, a mortal who defies their fated lifespan may survive longer through sheer will. If the environment doesn't kill them, and if the mortal forces their heart to keep beating,[5] a blue-coloured Heavenly Tribulation will descend to kill the mortal.[6] Should the mortal survive the tribulation, they will age rapidly and die after 49 days[7] (referencing the Cheondojae, 49 days of mourning in Buddhism between death and reincarnation). Only Immortal Cultivation or the Heart Path can extend a mortal's lifespan.

Heavenly Tribulations (天劫)

Heavenly Tribulations are strikes of lightning that typically strike the cultivator attempting to advance their realm. They typically begin manifesting while advancing to the Nascent Soul stage, and increase in intensity as the cultivator's realm rises. A cultivator facing a Heavenly Tribulation may not receive external help, otherwise the heavens will be enraged and bring forth a more severe tribulation.

Enders are burdened with an additional blue-coloured tribulation for every tribulation during their advancement,[8] essentially doubling their number of tribulations. They also receive Heavenly Tribulations one stage early than others.[9] Some peculiar beings, such as Hong Fan who was born with an old soul, receive abnormally calamitous Heavenly Tribulations of titanic proportions — severe punishment in return of tremendous power and privileges.[10]

Certain techniques, such as the Consoling Lightning Ritual,[11] as long as done by "oneself", can help make the Heavenly Tribulation less severe. And those who practice Lightning Path cultivation methods[12][13] or powerful cultivators at the Entering Nirvana stage[14] may delay a Heavenly Tribulation by a small amount of time. Examples range from three days to a year, respectively.

Extraordinary Pattern Law Talent — Heavenly Penalty (天刑)

The Extraordinary Pattern Law Talent is a living fate born by great being theorized to be Bong Myeong. Beings who receive this living fate are born with the fate to 'become the greatest artisans within the land they dwell'. However, should they leave their land for whatever reason, they will die. This is their Heavenly Penalty.[15]

The reason leaving their land incurs their Heavenly Penalty is because they break free of their fate imposed upon them. Specifically, the fate that they're the greatest artisans 'within the land they dwell', whether it be a planet in the Astral Realm, Decaying Corpse Realm, or Middle Realm. There are two known Heavenly Penalty in the story, each from different Extraordinary Pattern Law Talent bloodlines. The first is tied to the surname of the Jo Clan that Mad Lord Jo Yeon hailed from. Mad Lord resisted his fate by connecting his heart with [Her] and forcibly tethered his soul to Bright Cold Realm using the ghostly energy of the Nether Crossing Ship's power source he stole.[16] Meanwhile, Buk Hyang-hwa's Heavenly Penalty gradually dulls her senses until they perish. Eventually, even her nervous system will be paralyzed, and she will forget how to breathe.[17]

The Extraordinary Pattern Law Talent was likened to 'a bird trapped in a cage' by Mad Lord.[16] It is likely that the being that birthed this living fate is connected to the [Bird Trapped In A Cage] symbol.

Heart Demon Tribulation (心魔劫)

When an Integration stage cultivator achieves Grand Perfection and attains all six Divine Powers, a Heart Demon Tribulation is added. These are Heart Heavenly Demons, masses of negative emotions and energies given personality like Heart Demons. They attack the heart essence of a cultivator from the six directions at the Plane of Soul.[18]
